# Physical AI Foundations Module Specification

## Module Overview
**Module Title**: Physical AI Foundations
**Duration**: 20 hours
**Prerequisites**: Basic understanding of linear algebra, calculus, and programming concepts
**Learning Objectives**:
- Understand the fundamental principles of Physical AI and embodied intelligence
- Learn how physical interaction shapes intelligent behavior
- Explore the relationship between perception, action, and learning in physical systems

## Content Structure
### 1. Theory Section
Physical AI represents a paradigm shift from traditional AI that operates in abstract digital spaces to AI systems that interact with and learn from physical environments. This module covers:

- Definition and scope of Physical AI
- Embodied cognition principles
- Sensorimotor learning
- Physics-aware machine learning
- Control theory fundamentals
- Multi-modal perception in physical contexts

### 2. Practical Application
Students will engage with:
- Simulation environments demonstrating physical interaction
- Basic robot control using ROS 2
- Sensor fusion exercises
- Simple manipulation tasks

### 3. Assessment Criteria
Students will demonstrate understanding through:
- Written examination of Physical AI concepts
- Practical implementation of sensorimotor tasks
- Analysis of embodied intelligence examples

## Resources
- **Required Reading**:
  - "Embodied Intelligence" by Pfeifer & Bongard
  - Recent papers on Physical AI from RSS and ICRA conferences
- **Software Dependencies**:
  - ROS 2 Humble Hawksbill
  - Gazebo simulation environment
  - Python 3.8+ and relevant ML libraries
- **Hardware Requirements**:
  - Access to robotic platform (real or simulated)
